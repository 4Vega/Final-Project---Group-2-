Welcome to Cybersecurity Basics

This site is designed for beginners to understand how to stay safe online and learn about IT and cybersecurity careers. Whether you're a student, parent, or curious about the internet, we provide simple, beginner-friendly guides to get you started.

Quick Tips for Online Safety

Password Safety

Create strong, unique passwords and use a password manager.

Think Before You Click

Avoid suspicious links and unknown attachments to prevent phishing.

Keep Software Updated

Always update your devices and applications to fix security vulnerabilities.

Common Cyber Threats

These are some of the most common ways attackers try to trick or harm users online.

Phishing

Fake messages that try to trick you into giving away passwords or personal information.

Malware

Harmful software that can steal data, slow down your device, or spy on what you do.

Ransomware

Malware that locks your files and demands payment to unlock them.

Social Engineering

Attackers who pretend to be trusted people to get you to share information or give access.

Credential Stuffing

Using passwords stolen from one website to break into your other accounts.

How to Protect Yourself

Use this checklist to build safer online habits.

  • Use a different password for important accounts like email, banking, and school.
  • Turn on two factor authentication for email and social media whenever possible.
  • Keep your phone, laptop, and apps updated to patch security flaws.
  • Review app permissions and remove apps you do not use anymore.
  • Do not share personal details like full birthdate or address on public profiles.
  • Back up important files to a cloud drive or external drive regularly.

What Would You Do?

Read each situation and choose what you would do. The correct choice turns green and the wrong one turns red.

You receive an email that looks like it is from your bank saying there was “unusual activity” and you must log in using the link in the email. The sender address is slightly misspelled and the link looks unfamiliar. Should you log in using the link?

Your web browser shows a notification inside its settings that a security update is available. You opened the settings yourself and the update is coming from the browser, not a random website. Should you install the update?

A new website offers you a discount if you reuse the same password you already use for school and your main email. This sounds convenient and easy to remember. Is it a good idea to reuse that password?

Helpful Security Tools

These tools can help you stay safer with less effort.

Password Manager

Stores your passwords securely and lets you create long, unique passwords for each site.

Antivirus and Anti Malware

Scans your device for harmful software and helps block dangerous downloads.

Two Factor Apps

Authenticator apps add a one time code so attackers cannot log in with only your password.

Secure Backup

Cloud storage or external drives protect your files if your device is lost, stolen, or hacked.
